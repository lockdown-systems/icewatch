About ICEWatch

Under Section 216 of The Fiscal Year 2020 Department of Homeland Security Appropriations Bill, the US government is required to publish weekly data on the population of individuals detained by ICE, including the facilities where they are being held.

The data is quietly published on the ICE Website, where if you scroll to the bottom, you'll find a link to an Excel spreadsheet. That spreadsheet shows you who is detained in ICE facilities, at the time of this writing, over 47,000 people â€“ the vast majority of whom have no criminal records. This is despite the rhetoric from the Trump administration of targeting "the worst of the worst".

Our dashboard shows each immigration detention center and their population breakdown: whether they are considered "non-criminal" or "criminal", and their "ICE Threat Level". ICE's own data shows that they are detaining people who apparently pose no threat at all.

This project was inspired by this Instagram Reel by journalist Gustavo Solis, who first highlighted this dataset and its importance.
